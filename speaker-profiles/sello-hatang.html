Sello Hatang

CEO • Nelson Mandela Foundation

Sello Hatang joined the Nelson Mandela Foundation in 2009 and later served as Chief Executive Officer for about a decade. His career has moved through information and communications, human rights advocacy and archival work, with leadership roles at the South African Human Rights Commission, the South African History Archive and the Department of Arts, Culture, Science and Technology. He has also contributed to Mandela-related publications and serves on governance and democracy-focused boards.

Profile overview

Perspective

History becomes powerful when it is used to build accountability, public voice and practical change.

Background

Sello Hatang joined the Nelson Mandela Foundation in 2009 as Manager of Information Communications and became Chief Executive Officer in 2013, serving in that role for about a decade. Before that, he was Head of Information Communications and spokesperson for the South African Human Rights Commission from 2005 to 2009, Director of the South African History Archive from 2002 to 2005, and a Project Archivist at the Department of Arts, Culture, Science and Technology from 1998 to 2002. He also contributed to Mandela-related publications, including Conversations with Myself and Nelson Mandela by Himself.

Organisation

Nelson Mandela Foundation — The Nelson Mandela Foundation advances Mandela's legacy through memory, dialogue and public engagement, connecting historical reflection with present-day social justice work.
